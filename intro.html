octoroute was obtained by combining octo-, a prefix meaning eight in Latin (ὀκτώ in Ancient Greek), and route derived in early XIII century from Old French rute, meaning "road, way, path" (rupta (via), "(a road) opened by force" in Latin).

octoroute could then be translated as an "eightfold path."
The Noble Eightfold Path, is one of the key teachings of Siddhārtha Gautama, a spiritual teacher and founder of Buddhism. The Noble Eightfold Path teaches moderation leading to the cessation of suffering, the achievement of self-awakening, leading to the ultimate freedom of Nirvana. This path meets the needs of the body without crossing over into luxury or indulgence. It is also very important that it rejects ascetism as an impediment to freedom from suffering. Sometimes known as the Middle Path or the Middle Way, the Noble Eightfold Path is a way to develop insight into the true nature of reality and life free of greed, hatred, and delusion.
